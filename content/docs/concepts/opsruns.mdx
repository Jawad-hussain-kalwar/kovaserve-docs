**OpsRuns** is the managed-agent SKU for IT, cloud, and security operations — incident investigations, log analysis, runbook execution, infrastructure diagnostics, and policy-controlled remediation. An OpsRun receives an operational task, pulls the relevant logs and metrics, walks the runbook, calls the diagnostic tools, gates any remediation behind explicit policy, recovers from intermediate failures, respects budget, and produces an auditable outcome.

OpsRuns is the SKU that proves the platform on workloads where **policy and audit are non-negotiable** — where the wrong tool call has business consequences and every action needs to be explainable to a security or compliance reviewer.

## What an OpsRun does

The reference OpsRun flow:

1. **Receive a signal.** An alert from a monitoring system, an incident ticket, a security event, or an explicit operator prompt arrives at the OpsRun's dispatch endpoint.
2. **Correlate.** The agent pulls the relevant context — recent log windows, alert history, related tickets, runbook references, asset metadata — through the tool gateway, with every fetch governed by tool policy.
3. **Walk the runbook.** Operations work is procedure-driven. The agent follows the registered runbook step by step, recording every step on the timeline and dropping checkpoints between phases.
4. **Diagnose.** Diagnostic tools — log greppers, metric queriers, asset inspectors, network probes — run inside the sandbox or are dispatched to read-only services. Results land as artifacts.
5. **Gate remediation.** Any state-changing action — restart a service, kill a process, rotate a credential, modify a security group — passes through tool policy first. Actions outside the agent's allowed surface are blocked; actions on the policy-gated surface require explicit approval (operator-in-the-loop) or an explicit checkpoint advisory.
6. **Recover.** A failed step does not lose the investigation. The OpsRun resumes from the last checkpoint, paying only for the new work.
7. **Complete.** The OpsRun ends with a structured outcome — root cause, evidence trail, actions taken (if any), recommendations, and a full policy audit.

The point of an OpsRun is *not* automation for its own sake. It is **governed automation** — the kinds of investigations and remediations a team would otherwise do by hand, performed reliably under explicit budget and policy with an auditable trail.

## Use cases

| Use case | What the OpsRun does |
|---|---|
| Incident investigation | Pulls the alert context, correlates with recent deployments and incidents, queries logs and metrics, surfaces the most likely cause and the evidence behind it. |
| Log analysis | Searches across log streams for a specified pattern or signature, reduces hits to a structured summary, attaches sample lines as artifacts, optionally opens a follow-up investigation. |
| Runbook execution | Walks a registered runbook step by step, executes each step's tooling under policy, gates any state-changing action behind explicit approval, produces a runbook-completion record. |
| Infrastructure diagnostics | Inspects assets — VMs, clusters, queues, databases — against a diagnostic checklist, surfaces anomalies with evidence, recommends actions without taking them by default. |
| Policy-controlled remediation | Performs scoped remediation — restart a stuck pod, expire a stale lock, rotate a credential, apply a runbook patch — only when the action is on the agent's allowed surface and the policy decision permits it. |

## What OpsRuns is built on

| Layer | OpsRuns default |
|---|---|
| Runtime | The KovaServe managed runtime — workers, sandboxes, tool gateway, runtime-native timeline. |
| Workspace | Optional. Many OpsRuns operate against external observability surfaces and do not need a writable filesystem; runbook-execution OpsRuns may bind a workspace to a runbook repository. |
| Sandbox profile | Container image with diagnostic toolchains (curl, jq, kubectl in read-only mode, log-fetcher clients). Network policy targets the customer's observability and asset surfaces only — no general internet egress. |
| Tool catalog | Log query, metric query, alert/ticket lookup, asset inspector, runbook reader, structured diagnostics, and a separate **remediation** catalog whose tools require policy approval per invocation. |
| Default budget | Bounded cost cap, bounded step cap, time cap appropriate for an investigation window — typically tighter than CodeRuns. |
| Default context budget | Tuned for log-heavy contexts: aggressive offload of raw log payloads to artifact storage, summary-required disposition on bulky tool outputs, compact-required threshold to keep the working context lean across long investigations. |
| Default policy scope | Tool policy that defaults remediation tools to *blocked* unless an explicit policy decision allows them. Data boundary set to the customer's observability region. Audit retention extended for compliance. |
| Checkpoint trigger rules | Before every remediation tool invocation, after every correlation phase, on every budget warning, before every runbook gate, manually on operator request. |
| Observability | Runtime-native timeline with a foregrounded **policy audit pane** — every governed action and the policy decision that produced it. Every artifact (log slice, metric query result, runbook step record) is downloadable. |

## How a customer dispatches an OpsRun

OpsRuns are typically wired into the customer's existing alerting and incident systems. An incident webhook hits the operator's backend, which dispatches into KovaServe:

```http
POST /v1/ks/runtime/runs
Authorization: Bearer ks_...
Content-Type: application/json

{
  "agent_id":     "01J8X9Y2Z3K4M5N6P7Q8R9OPA1",
  "session_id":   "ses_a7f3c2b8d4e1f0925a1b6c3d",
  "inputs": {
    "incident_id":  "INC-2026-04-30-0042",
    "summary":      "Payment service P95 latency above SLO since 11:30 UTC.",
    "asset_refs":   ["svc/payments", "db/payments-primary"],
    "alert_window": { "start": "2026-04-30T11:00:00Z", "end": "2026-04-30T12:00:00Z" }
  },
  "budget":          { "cost_cents_limit": 1500, "time_seconds_limit": 1800 },
  "context_budget":  { "active_token_limit": 64000 },
  "policy_scope":    "ops/level-1",
  "checkpoint_policy": { "before_remediation": true, "between_runbook_steps": true }
}
```

The OpsRun streams progress on the runtime timeline. The operator's incident UI surfaces the agent's findings, the evidence, the policy decisions, and any remediation actions taken — alongside the human-on-call's own actions.

## What a customer gets back

When an OpsRun reaches `COMPLETED`:

- A **structured outcome** — root cause if identified, evidence with citations to the source logs/metrics, actions taken (with the policy decision attached to each), and recommendations for the human-on-call.
- A **runbook execution record** — every runbook step the agent walked, the tool calls per step, the artifacts captured, the gates that passed or required approval.
- An **evidence pack** — the log slices, metric query results, asset snapshots, and tool outputs the agent referenced, retained per the deployment's audit retention setting.
- A **policy audit** — every governed action and the policy decision behind it, suitable for compliance review.
- A **cost per completed run** number — what it cost to complete the investigation or the remediation, with frozen pricing snapshots.

If the OpsRun was unable to complete (budget hit, policy denied, evidence insufficient), the partial work is the same shape — the agent's diagnoses, the evidence captured, the gate that stopped it, and the next-step recommendation for a human. *Failed* on the platform side and *unable to safely conclude* on the operations side are surfaced distinctly so the on-call human knows whether to re-run with adjusted policy or to take it over manually.

## What policy and audit look like

OpsRuns is designed for environments where *every action must be explainable*. The platform supports that through three surfaces:

- **Per-action governance.** Every tool invocation produces a policy decision recorded on the timeline — allow, allow-with-degradation, allow-with-checkpoint-advisory, deny. Remediation tools default to deny unless explicitly allowed for the agent's policy scope.
- **Operator-in-the-loop gates.** Specific tool invocations or runbook steps can require explicit operator approval before proceeding. The OpsRun pauses at the gate and resumes when approval is recorded.
- **Audit retention.** OpsRun timelines, evidence packs, and policy decisions are retained under the data-boundary and audit retention configured on the deployment's policy scope. The export endpoint produces a one-line-per-record JSONL stream suitable for SIEM ingestion or compliance archive.

## What OpsRuns is not

- **Not auto-remediation by default.** OpsRuns *can* remediate, but only when the policy scope explicitly allows it. The default is read-only diagnosis with recommendations.
- **Not a logging product.** OpsRuns reads logs and metrics from the customer's existing observability surfaces; it does not replace them.
- **Not a SOAR replacement.** OpsRuns covers the agent execution and governance surface. Customers with existing SOAR or runbook automation can dispatch OpsRuns from inside those systems and feed outcomes back.
- **Not unbounded investigation.** Every OpsRun runs under a budget, a context budget, and a tool policy. A diagnostic that goes nowhere ends within its budget; a remediation that hits a gate stops at the gate.
